Preventing Repetitive Strain Injury

Published on November 3, 2025

Repetitive strain injury (RSI) is a common problem for people who type a lot. Here's how to prevent it.

1. Use Good Posture

As we've discussed in other posts, good posture is the first line of defense against RSI.

2. Take Frequent Breaks

Get up and move around every 30 minutes to an hour.

3. Stretch Your Hands and Wrists

Regular stretching can help relieve tension and prevent injuries.

4. Use an Ergonomic Keyboard and Mouse

Ergonomic peripherals can help reduce strain on your hands and wrists.

5. Listen to Your Body

If you feel pain, stop what you're doing and take a break. Don't try to push through the pain.
