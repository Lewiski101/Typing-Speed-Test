Beginner's Guide to Touch Typing

Published on November 3, 2025

Touch typing is the ability to type without looking at the keyboard. It's a fundamental skill for anyone who uses a computer regularly. This guide will walk you through the basics of touch typing and set you on the path to becoming a faster, more efficient typist.

The Home Row

The foundation of touch typing is the home row. For your left hand, these are the A, S, D, and F keys. For your right hand, they are J, K, L, and the semicolon (;). Your fingers should always return to these keys after pressing any other key.

Proper Finger Placement

Each finger is responsible for a specific set of keys. This division of labor is what makes touch typing so efficient. There are many charts and tutorials online that show the correct finger-to-key mapping.

Practice, Practice, Practice

Learning touch typing takes time and dedication. Start with simple exercises, focusing on accuracy rather than speed. As you become more comfortable, you can gradually increase your speed. Use online typing tutors and games to make practice more engaging.
